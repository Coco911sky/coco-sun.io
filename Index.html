Spring Festival

The Spring Festival, the lunar new year, is the beginning of the year and the traditional festival. It is commonly known as the new year, the new year, the new year, the new year's day, the new year's year, the new year's day, the new year's year, and so on. It is also known orally as the new year's day, the new year's day, the new year's Eve, and the new year's day. The Spring Festival has a long history, which evolved from the worship of praying for new year in ancient times. All things are based on heaven and people are based on ancestors. Praying for the new year, offering sacrifices to heaven and worshiping the ancestors are the same. The origin of the Spring Festival contains profound cultural connotation, which carries rich historical and cultural heritage in the process of inheritance and development. During the Spring Festival, various activities were held throughout the country, with strong regional characteristics and a lively atmosphere. These activities mainly included old cloth, exorcism, worship and worship, and rich and colorful forms.

The origin of Spring Festival

There is an old legend. In ancient China, there was a kind of animal called Nian. "Nian", with horns on his head, is very fierce and terrifying. "Nian" has lived in the mountains for many years. He climbs ashore on a specific day (that is, new year's Eve now), swallowing livestock and harming people's lives. Therefore, every new year's Eve, all the people in each village fled to the mountains to avoid the harm of "Nian". On New Year's Eve, there was an old man begging from outside. The village was in a hurry and panic, and no one paid any attention to him. Only an old woman in the east of the village gave the old man some food and advised him to go up the mountain to avoid Nian. The old man raised his beard and said with a smile, "if my mother-in-law wants me to stay at home all night, I will drive Nian away." In the middle of the night, Nian rushes into the village. It's different from the old village: the atmosphere in the village is red. Nian shuddered and screamed. Near the door, there was a "crackling" explosion in the courtyard. Nian was so scared that he didn't dare to move forward. It turns out that Nian is most afraid of red, fire and explosion. At this time, the door of her mother-in-law's house opened and an old man in a red robe was laughing. "Nian" was shocked and ran away. The next day was the first day of the first month. The people who came back from the refuge were very surprised to see that the village was safe and sound. At this time, the old woman quickly told the villagers about the promise of the old beggar. This matter soon spread in the surrounding villages, and people knew the way to drive out Nian. Since then, every year on New Year's Eve, every family pasted red couplets and set off firecrackers; every family had bright candles and waited for the new year. In the early morning of the first day of junior high school, I still have to go to my relatives and friends to say hello. This custom is widely spread and has become the most solemn traditional festival among Chinese people, that is, the Spring Festival. According to historical records, the Spring Festival was called Zai in Tang and Yu dynasties, Sui in Xia Dynasty, Si in Shang Dynasty and Nian in Zhou Dynasty.

The custom of Spring Festival

1.As the biggest and most important festival in China, Spring Festival must have many customs. New year's Eve New year's Eve refers to the night of the last day of the lunar month, which is connected with the first day of the first month. The word "except" in "New Year's Eve" means "go; night: alternation". New Year's Eve means "the month is poor and the year is over". People have to get rid of the old and welcome the new. When the old year comes, they will get rid of it and change the new year in the coming year. Therefore, the activities during this period are centered around eliminating the old and welcoming the new, eliminating disasters and praying for blessings. At the end of each year in the Zhou and Qin Dynasties, the ceremony of "Tiao Zhong Kui" and "Da Nuo" was held in the imperial palace to beat the drum to expel the evil spirits, which was called "ChuChu". Later, it was also called "Xiaochu" the day before New Year's Eve, that is, new year's Eve; new year's Eve was "Dachu", that is, new year's Eve. 2. Stick to the door god At first, the door god was carved in the shape of peach wood and hung next to people. Later, it was painted as a portrait of the door god and posted on the door. The two brothers, Shencha and Yulei, are said to be in charge of ghosts. They guard the door, and the big and small ghosts dare not harm them. After the Tang Dynasty, there were also pictures of Qin Qiong and Yuchi Jingde as door gods, and pictures of Guan Yu and Zhang Fei as door gods. There is one door god on the left and one on the right. Later generations often paint a pair of door gods as a man and a martial arts, which embodies the good wishes of the working people of the Han nationality to ward off evil spirits and disasters, welcome auspicious and accept good fortune. 3. Paste Spring Festival couplets Spring Festival couplets are also called "door couplets", "couplets", "Taofu" and so on. They are a kind of couplets and get their names because they are posted during the Spring Festival. It describes the background of the times and expresses good wishes in neat, dual, concise and exquisite words, which is a unique literary form in China. Every Spring Festival, whether in urban or rural areas, every household should select a red couplet and paste it on the door to increase the festive atmosphere. This custom began in the Song Dynasty and became popular in the Ming Dynasty. In the Qing Dynasty, the ideological and artistic quality of spring couplets had been greatly improved. Liang Zhangju's spring couplets monograph "chuanglianghua" discussed the origin of couplets and the characteristics of various works. spring There are many kinds of couplets, which can be divided into door center, frame pair, horizontal drape, spring stripe, Doufang, etc. "Door center" is pasted on the center of the upper part of the door panel; "frame pair" is pasted on the left and right door frames; "horizontal drape" is pasted on the transverse wood of the lintel; "spring strip" is pasted on the corresponding places according to different contents; "Doufang" is also called "door leaf", which is a square diamond, and is mostly pasted on furniture and screen walls. 4. Paste New Year pictures Spring Festival New Year pictures are also very common in urban and rural areas. The black and colorful new year pictures add a lot of prosperous and happy atmosphere to thousands of families. All these have the folk function of praying and decorating residence. New Year picture is an ancient folk art in China. It reflects the customs and beliefs of the people and hopes for the future. Like the Spring Festival couplets, it originated from "door god". With the rise of wood printing, the content of New Year pictures is not only limited to the monotonous themes such as door god, but also becomes rich and colorful. In some new year painting workshops, classic color New Year pictures such as "three stars of fortune and longevity", "blessing of heavenly officials", "bumper harvest of five grains", "flourishing of six animals" and "blessing in spring" have been produced to meet people's good wishes of celebrating and praying for the new year There are three important producing areas: Taohuawu in Suzhou, Yangliuqing in Tianjin and Weifang in Shandong. The earliest collection of New Year pictures in China today is the woodcut New Year pictures of the Southern Song Dynasty, which depicts four ancient beauties: Wang Zhaojun, Zhao Feiyan, ban Ji and Lvzhu. The most popular one of the new year's pictures is a mouse. It depicts the interesting scene that the mouse marries the bride according to the customs of the world. In the early years of the Republic of China, Zheng mantuo in Shanghai combined lunar calendar with New Year pictures. This is a new form of New Year pictures. This kind of two in one new year picture, later developed into a calendar, is still popular throughout the country today. 5. Shou Sui Chinese people have the habit of keeping watch on New Year's Eve, commonly known as "aonian". ShouSui starts with the new year's Eve dinner, which should be eaten slowly. Some people have to eat until late at night. New year's Eve is one of the most important new year's customs, which has a long history. The earliest records can be found in the local records of ZhouChu in the Western Jin Dynasty: on New Year's Eve, each person gives gifts to others, which is called "giving new year's Day"; when wine and food are invited to each other, which is called "parting New Year's Day"; when the elders and children get together to drink, which is called "sharing new year's Day"; when everyone stays up all night, waiting for the dawn, which is called "keeping New Year's Day". Since the Han Dynasty, the time of the alternation of new year and old year is generally in the middle of the night. 6. Get up early Some people think that if they don't get up early, the fields will collapse. So get up early. 7. Open fire Spring Festival morning, open the door auspicious, first set off firecrackers, called "open the door firecracker.". After the sound of firecrackers, the ground is full of broken red, as bright as brocade, which is called "full house red". At this time, the street was full of auspicious spirit and joy. 8. New year's greetings One of the important activities in the Spring Festival is to celebrate the new year at the homes of relatives, friends and neighbors. In the Han Dynasty, new year's greetings were popular. After the Tang and Song Dynasties, it was very popular. If it was inconvenient to go there in person, you can use a famous post to celebrate. In the Eastern Han Dynasty, it was called "Ci", so business card was also called "Mingci". After the Ming Dynasty, many people pasted a red paper bag at the door to collect famous posters, called "menbo". 9. New year's money

New year's money (called "balise" in Guangdong) is given to the younger generation by the elder. In some families, after dinner, everyone sits at the table and is not allowed to go. When everyone has finished eating, the elder will give it to the younger generation, and encourage their children and grandchildren to learn and improve in the new year. In some families, parents give their children new year's money under their pillows at night after their children fall asleep, which shows the elder's love and respect for the younger generation. It is a folk activity integrating family ethics.
